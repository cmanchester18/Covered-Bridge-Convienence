Our Story

A family business
three decades strong

It all started as the Covered Bridge Cafe, operating out of Moore's Market — founded by Art and Christine Egan and their son Mike Manchester. In 2001, their permanent home on Route 29 was built by local craftsman John Mowers, right here in the heart of Salisbury Center.

Since 2016, the business has been proudly owned and operated by Mike & Heather Manchester. On any given day, you're likely to see one of their four children — Greg, Kiersten, Alyson, or Cameron — working alongside them. It's a true family affair, every single day.

"A clean, comfortable, fun place for both our local and seasonal guests to gather."
— Mike & Heather Manchester, Owners

Named for a landmark built in 1875

A short walk from our door stands the Salisbury Center Covered Bridge — built by Alvah Hopson, spanning Spruce Creek, and listed on the National Register of Historic Places. It's the only covered bridge remaining in Herkimer County, appears on the town's official seal, and is described by visitors as "straight from a Hallmark movie." We carry its name with pride: built by community, for community.
